### **Question 1: Is your app restricted to users who are part of a single company?**

**Answer: No.**

BookMate is **NOT** restricted to users of a single company. BookMate is a consumer-facing personal finance app available to anyone in the world who wants to track their expenses.

**Target Audience:**
- Individual consumers managing personal budgets
- Freelancers tracking business expenses
- Self-employed professionals
- Students managing spending money
- Anyone wanting to organize their financial records

**No Company Affiliation Required:**
- Users do not need to be employed by, affiliated with, or contracted to any specific organization
- Any person can download BookMate from the App Store and use it immediately
- There are no company verification checks, invitation codes, or enterprise requirements

---

### **Question 2: Is your app designed for use by a limited or specific group of companies?**

**Answer: No.**

BookMate is **NOT** designed for any group of companies, limited or otherwise. BookMate is designed for **individual users** managing their personal or freelance finances.

**Key Points:**
- BookMate is a personal expense tracker, like Mint, YNAB, or Expensify (consumer versions)
- While small business owners may use it, they use it as individuals managing their own finances, not as part of a corporate system
- There are no multi-user features, team collaboration tools, or company-wide deployment capabilities
- No corporate accounts, no admin portals, no company dashboards

**Distribution:**
- BookMate is intended for public distribution on the App Store
- Any individual user can download it
- No enterprise licensing or company partnerships required

---

### **Question 3: What features in the app, if any, are intended for use by the general public?**

**Answer: ALL features are intended for the general public.**

Every feature in BookMate is designed for personal use by individual consumers:

#### **1. Receipt Upload & OCR Scanning**
- **What it does:** Users take photos of receipts (grocery store, restaurant, gas station, etc.)
- **OCR technology:** Automatically extracts amount, date, merchant name
- **Who uses it:** Any person who wants to digitize and organize their receipts
- **Public use case:** "I bought groceries, let me snap a photo and track this expense"

#### **2. Manual Expense Entry**
- **What it does:** Quick 3-step wizard to manually log expenses
- **Categories:** Personal categories (Food, Transport, Utilities, Entertainment, etc.)
- **Who uses it:** Anyone who paid cash or wants to log an expense without a receipt
- **Public use case:** "I paid 500 THB cash for taxi, let me log it quickly"

#### **3. Expense Categorization**
- **What it does:** Organize expenses by category, property/account, payment method
- **Flexibility:** User chooses categories that fit their personal needs
- **Who uses it:** Any individual tracking where their money goes
- **Public use case:** "How much did I spend on dining out this month?"

#### **4. Dashboard & Reports**
- **What it does:** Visual charts showing spending trends over time
- **Reports:** Monthly summaries, year-to-date totals, balance tracking
- **Who uses it:** Anyone who wants insights into their spending habits
- **Public use case:** "Am I spending too much on entertainment?"

#### **5. Activity History**
- **What it does:** View all past transactions in chronological order
- **Filtering:** Filter by date, category, amount
- **Who uses it:** Anyone who wants to review their financial history
- **Public use case:** "When did I pay my electric bill last month?"

#### **6. Cloud Backup**
- **What it does:** Syncs user's expense data to Google Sheets (user's personal account)
- **Privacy:** Each user has their own isolated data storage
- **Who uses it:** Anyone who wants their data backed up and accessible from other devices
- **Public use case:** "I want my receipts saved even if I lose my phone"

#### **7. Offline Mode**
- **What it does:** App works without internet connection, queues transactions for later sync
- **Who uses it:** Any user in areas with poor connectivity
- **Public use case:** "I'm traveling and don't have WiFi, but I still want to track expenses"

**Summary:** These are standard personal finance features found in consumer apps like Mint, YNAB, PocketGuard, and Expensify. Nothing is enterprise-specific.

### **Question 4: How do users obtain an account?**

**Answer: Currently no account required. Future versions will offer free sign-up directly in the app.**

#### **Current Version (v1.0.1 - Submitted):**

**No authentication required.** We intentionally launched v1.0 without requiring users to create accounts to reduce friction for first-time users.

**How it works:**
1. User downloads BookMate from App Store
2. User opens app
3. User immediately starts tracking expenses
4. Data is stored locally on device and synced to user's personal Google Sheets (if they choose to connect)

**Why we did this:**
- Lower barrier to entry for new users
- Users can try the app without commitment
- Common pattern for v1.0 of consumer apps (iterate based on feedback)

#### **Future Version (v1.1 - In Development):**

**Free account creation directly in the app.**

**Sign-up flow:**
1. User downloads BookMate from App Store
2. User opens app
3. User sees two options:
   - **"Create Account"** (new users)
   - **"Continue as Guest"** (try without account)
4. If "Create Account":
   - User enters email address
   - User creates password
   - User verifies email (optional)
   - Account created instantly
5. User starts using BookMate

**Important:**
- ‚úÖ **No company affiliation check**
- ‚úÖ **No invitation code required**
- ‚úÖ **No approval process**
- ‚úÖ **No corporate email domain verification**
- ‚úÖ **Anyone with an email can sign up**

This is identical to how Mint, YNAB, and other consumer finance apps handle sign-up.

### **Question 5: Is there any paid content in the app and if so who pays for it?**

**Answer: Currently 100% free. Future premium features will be paid by individual users via In-App Purchase.**

#### **Current Version (v1.0.1):**

**Completely FREE.** No paid content, no subscriptions, no in-app purchases.

**All features are free:**
- Unlimited receipt uploads
- Unlimited expense tracking
- Full dashboard and reports
- Cloud backup
- All features accessible to every user

**Why it's free:**
- We're building an audience and gathering user feedback
- Proving value before asking for payment
- Common launch strategy for consumer apps

#### **Future Monetization (v2.0+):**

**Freemium model with optional premium subscription.**

**Free Tier (Always Available):**
- 100 receipts per month
- Basic expense tracking
- Standard reports
- Core features

**Premium Tier ($4.99/month):**
- Unlimited receipts
- Advanced analytics
- Export to PDF/Excel
- Priority customer support
- Multi-device sync

**Who Pays:**
- **Individual users** pay for their own subscription
- Payment via **Apple In-App Purchase** (standard consumer payment)
- NOT paid by companies or organizations
- NOT corporate billing or enterprise licensing

**Examples of similar pricing:**
- Mint Premium: $4.99/month (consumer subscription)
- YNAB: $14.99/month (consumer subscription)
- Expensify Personal: Free (consumer tier)

**Key Point:** BookMate will use the **same business model as other consumer finance apps** - individual users choose to pay for premium features if they want them.
